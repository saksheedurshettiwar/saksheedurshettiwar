02
ATS / Recruitment CRM Platforms
Agency-facing tools. The backbone of how staffing agencies manage candidates and client relationships. Strong at placement workflow. Weak at structural compliance enforcement.
Bullhorn
22 of top 25 agencies · 10K+ firms
ATS + CRM · Agency-facing · Healthcare module
What it does well
End-to-end placement workflow — submission, credentialing, timekeeping, billing
Compliance Manager (2023) — credential status dashboard and checklist per placement
100+ marketplace integrations — credentialing tools bolt on
Real user complaints — Capterra 2024/2025
"Analytics feature is non-functional — admitted implementing the wrong version"
"One of the most expensive platforms on the market — not worth the price"
"Did mass layoffs because they are losing market share" — Oct 2025
The gap
Compliance Manager "shows" and "tracks" — Bullhorn's own docs confirm it cannot structurally block activation with open gaps. A checklist a coordinator can click past is not a gate.
NexusForce advantage
Architectural gate — system cannot activate a placement with open gaps. Not policy, not reminder, not checklist. Structurally blocked.
Ceipal Healthcare
Mid-market · ATS + compliance
ATS with Joint Commission compliance automation
What it does well
Built-in JC standard tracking — closest to compliance-aware in ATS space
Automated renewal reminders before credential expiry
Real user complaints — G2 + Capterra 2024
"Packets don't work the way they were explained — sought clarification twice"
"System is still very manual with data entry — dashboards not user-friendly"
"CVO and software teams do not coordinate well"
The gap
Reminder-based. A placement can be activated with open gaps — the system warns but does not block. No hospital portal. Hospital has zero compliance visibility.
NexusForce advantage
Hospital gets real-time compliance visibility in their own portal — not managed by the agency, not off-platform. Gaps visible to all parties simultaneously.
03
On-Demand Staffing Platforms
Mobile-first, speed-first. Built for per diem and shift-filling. Credentialing happens once at onboarding — not per placement against specific hospital requirements.
ShiftMed
$250M raised · Series D 2023
Mobile-first · W-2 model · 250K+ credentialed clinicians · 631K shifts (2025)
What it does well
W-2 model — reduces compliance burden for facilities vs independent contractors
AI-driven shift matching against pre-credentialed pool — fast for per diem
$189M in documented labour savings for health systems in 2025
Real user complaints — App Store 2024
"Bot sent me a link to a different state. After going back and forth, I gave up." — nurse lost placement
"There are NO live representatives, no customer support — for healthcare you need someone to contact"
The gap
Credentialing verified once at signup — not per placement against a specific ICU's requirements. Cannot enforce hospital-specific compliance packs. Bot-first UX means credentialing errors go unresolved.
NexusForce advantage
Per-placement compliance pack tied to specific hospital + specialty + location. The wallet carries forward what's already verified — but gaps for this specific job surface clearly.
IntelyCare
Acquired CareRev Dec 2025
Per diem marketplace · LPN/RN · 30+ states · W-2 employee model
What it does well
Full W-2 benefits — insurance, retirement, same-day pay ("IntelyPros")
Strong nurse retention model — treats clinicians as employees, not gig workers
Acquired CareRev December 2025 — expanding market reach aggressively
The gap
Per diem focused — not designed for 13-week travel contracts or multi-vendor managed service layer. No structured compliance gate per placement. No hospital portal with compliance visibility.
NexusForce advantage
Designed for complex multi-week contracts, not shift-fill. Three-party structure (hospital + agency + nurse) with separate data boundaries enforced architecturally.
Clipboard Health
Well-funded · LTC focused
Per diem marketplace · LTC / SNF · Transparent pay · Speed-first
What it does well
Transparent pay — nurses see full rate before accepting any shift
Fast — sub-hour matching for per diem LTC shifts
Real user complaints — BBB 2024
"Deactivated my account while I was laying my sister to rest — I did provide documentation"
"Supervisors do not respond whatsoever to concerns — they have several lawsuits"
The gap
One flat credential set for all facilities — an ICU has same requirements as an LTC on this platform. Not true in reality. Automated deactivation with no human escalation path. No audit trail a hospital could use in a JC review.
NexusForce advantage
Hospital-specific compliance pack per job posting. Requirements differ by specialty, location, and hospital policy. NexusForce enforces those differences structurally.
SnapCare
TIARA Best Use of Tech 2024
AI-enabled workforce marketplace · Float pool + contract staffing · Acquired Medecipher 2024
What it does well
Transparent Pricing Promise — discloses clinician pay + burden rate on invoices
Shift escalation strategy — internal staff first, outside agencies only when necessary
MSP option — consolidated billing and compliance across suppliers
The gap
Compliance is a feature layer, not a structural gate. Per-placement compliance enforcement against hospital-specific packs is not documented anywhere in their product.
NexusForce advantage
Immutability Rule + Snapshot Rule create a legally defensible audit trail that a SnapCare MSP layer cannot replicate — the record is timestamped and tamper-proof at activation.

symplr CTM: native credentialing engine applying discipline + facility-specific requirements
Real-time compliance dashboard — closest to visibility across parties in this category
symplr Access: vendor credentialing with smart badge check-in — 3M+ check-ins/year
Real user complaints — G2 + Capterra 2024
"Packets don't work the way they were explained — sought clarification twice"
"Very manual with data entry — dashboards not user-friendly"
"CVO and software teams do not coordinate well"

Four portals — all four parties see the same compliance status simultaneously. No manual data entry — documents uploaded by the party responsible, verified by admin, synced instantly to all portals.

VMS Platforms (Vendor Management Systems)
The middleware between hospitals and staffing agencies. Manages multi-vendor relationships, rate management, timekeeping. Closest structural cousin to NexusForce — but compliance is still tracking, not blocking.
Symplr CTM
Part of Symplr enterprise · Healthcare-VMS focused
VMS for healthcare staffing · Credentialing + timekeeping + payroll + billing
What it does well
Native credentialing engine — applies discipline + facility-specific requirements per placement
Geofenced clock-ins — validates shift location to reduce fraud + billing disputes
Mobile portals for both professionals and facilities
Real-time compliance dashboards — visibility into expiration status
The gap
Compliance "visibility" is not enforcement. Symplr CTM manages compliance status — but cannot structurally block placement activation when gaps are open. The credentialing engine tracks; it does not gate.
NexusForce advantage
Immutability Rule — the activation step itself is gated. Symplr CTM can show gaps; NexusForce prevents activation until gaps are closed. These are architecturally different.
Medefis
Part of larger healthcare ecosystem
Vendor-neutral VMS · Largest vendor + clinician pool · Real-time compliance tracking
What it does well
Vendor-neutral — facilities access wide range without single-vendor lock-in
Real-time compliance tracking and candidate status updates
Automated notifications for credential expirations
The gap
Real-time tracking ≠ real-time enforcement. Notifications tell you something expired — they don't prevent the placement that should have been blocked. No nurse-facing wallet. No immutable activation record.

The White Space — What Nobody Has Built
After researching every platform in the market, six structural gaps remain unaddressed by every competitor. NexusForce is designed to close all six simultaneously.
⏰
Gap 01
No structural gate at activation
Every platform checks compliance after placement decision. None structurally block activation when gaps exist. Someone can always click past the warning.
🏗️
Gap 02
Rate separation is UI-only
Every platform claiming rate separation filters it in a view — the data is still in the DOM. No platform has removed vendor rates from the hospital's data model entirely.
📸
Gap 03
Template edits affect active placements
No platform freezes requirements at job creation. A template updated mid-contract can change what a nurse needs retroactively — a JC audit risk nobody has solved.
👛
Gap 04
Nurses re-submit the same documents forever
Bullhorn's own survey: "most would prefer a single place to update credentials." No platform has a portable wallet where approved documents carry forward automatically across placements.
👁️
Gap 05
No shared real-time compliance view
Hospital, agency, nurse, and admin all see different versions of compliance status — communicated by email and phone. No platform gives all four parties the same real-time picture.
🔒
Gap 06
No immutable audit trail at activation
JC requires a defensible audit trail. Yet no platform creates a timestamped, tamper-proof record at the exact moment of activation. Approvals sit in mutable databases — editable after the fact.

NexusForce's position in the market
The market has compliance tracking. Nobody has compliance enforcement. That's the white space.
After mapping 16 platforms across 6 categories — ATS, on-demand, marketplace, credentialing, VMS, and scheduling — no single platform closes all six gaps simultaneously. The closest is Symplr CTM, which has hospital + agency portals and per-placement compliance packs — but still cannot structurally block activation when gaps are open. The Snapshot Rule, Immutability Rule, Union Rule, and Rate Isolation are architecturally unique. That's not a feature comparison. That's a different class of product.
